Agent 2 — Strategic Analysis

Overall Score3.3 / 5
Key InsightDespite varying WTP and ethical stances, high prices, content unavailability, and convenience issues are universal drivers of digital piracy in Taiwan, with significant underreporting of actual piracy behavior across all segments due to social desirability bias.
Target SegmentUrban Tech-Savvy Professional
Recommendations
1Implement tiered pricing models, especially for Budget-conscious Students (WTP NT$352.5) and Value-Conscious Workers (WTP NT$267.5), potentially offering student/low-income discounts or freemium options to convert high-frequency pirates into legal subscribers, as cost is a universal barrier.
2Prioritize securing broader content licenses to address the 100% reported 'Content not available legally in Taiwan' driver across all segments, thereby removing a key justification for piracy.
3Enhance the convenience and user experience of legal platforms, particularly for streaming and software, to match or exceed the ease of access offered by unauthorized content, which is a 100% driver for students, tech-savvy professionals, and value-conscious workers.
4Develop bundled subscription packages for Family-Focused Parents (WTP NT$792.5) to address their concern about balancing the cost of multiple legal services, leveraging their high trust (5.0/5) and guilt (4.95/5) regarding piracy.
5Launch targeted educational campaigns for Budget-conscious Students and Value-Conscious Workers, focusing on the value proposition of legal content (currently rated 1.95/5 and 2.75/5 respectively) and the ethical implications, as these segments show the lowest ethical concerns and highest SDB.
Risk Flags
(!) The social desirability bias (SDB) gap is substantial across all segments, with Budget-conscious Students (+3.20) and Value-Conscious Workers (+3.05) showing the largest discrepancies between anonymous and named piracy disclosures. Actual piracy rates are significantly higher than self-reported figures in non-anonymous settings.
(!) The universal drivers of piracy — high price of legal options (100% across all segments) and content not available legally in Taiwan (100% across all segments) — present fundamental structural challenges that, if unaddressed, will continue to fuel unauthorized consumption regardless of segment-specific WTP or ethical considerations.

Audience Segments

Budget-conscious Studentweight: 25%
Persona: You are a 20-year-old university student in Taipei, living on a tight budget of NT$8,000/month from part-time work and family support. You use digital content daily — streaming, ebooks, software — but find legal options unaffordable. You casually pirate content without much guilt, seeing it as a practical necessity.
Rationale: Students are a high-frequency piracy segment with the lowest disposable income, making price sensitivity and SDB gap likely to be pronounced.
Urban Tech-Savvy Professionalweight: 25%
Persona: You are a 32-year-old software engineer in Taipei earning NT$120,000/month. You subscribe to multiple legal platforms (Netflix, Spotify, Adobe) but also occasionally use unauthorized tools. You're aware of copyright law and feel mild guilt about piracy, but prioritize convenience.
Rationale: High-income tech workers balance legal usage with occasional piracy; their SDB gap and WTP data benchmark the high end.
Family-Focused Parentweight: 25%
Persona: You are a 42-year-old parent of two in Taichung with a household income of NT$80,000/month. You subscribe to legal streaming for your family but worry about the cost of multiple services. You view piracy as setting a bad example and rarely engage in it.
Rationale: Parents represent a segment with high ethical concern and family-oriented content needs; expected low SDB gap and high guilt.
Value-Conscious Workerweight: 25%
Persona: You are a 28-year-old office worker in Kaohsiung earning NT$35,000/month. You regularly use pirated content to stretch your budget — streaming sites, cracked software — but would deny it in formal settings due to social pressure.
Rationale: Mid-income workers with high piracy behavior and strong social desirability effects; expected to show a large anonymous vs named gap.
